About us

Growing up, school was always a safe place for me. Some of my fondest childhood memories were shaped by loving and kind-hearted teachers, who fostered me to become a strong, independent, and confident student of life. Years later, when I became a parent and my own children entered school, I expected them to have a similar experience. I still believed that teachers would continue to impart their love for education onto my children, by providing them with their varying perspectives and insights to motivate them to be their best selves. Unfortunately, this was not always the case.

My boys are now in their teenage years, and we have been through pre-school, elementary school, middle school, and now high school. While they have had some amazing teachers, who have taught them priceless, life-enhancing lessons; they have also had teachers who caused some serious self-esteem issues and emotional distress that were difficult to overcome.

As a family, we do not dwell on negative experiences, but instead seek to learn from them, grow, and move on. About a year ago at my son’s school, an uncomfortable meeting with a teacher utilizing some “questionable” and aggressive teaching methods ended with her stating that she did not have to answer to ‘some parent’ (meaning me). She was not interested in trying to resolve the situation in the best interest of my student, or any of the other students who were being negatively impacted by her behavior.

After the shock of the meeting wore off, it dawned on me that while students have their performance measured all year round at school, a strong platform to provide feedback on teachers’ performance simply does not exist. For teachers to grow and offer an environment to our children that stimulates personal development and positive connections, feedback from students and parents on teachers’ performance is imperative.

Teachers play such an important role in the lives of our children. Classroom experiences, both positive and negative, affect and shape children, staying with them for the rest of their lives.

This platform aims to bridge the communication gap that currently exists among parents, students, and teachers. My wish is to create a positive flow of feedback for teachers from those people they directly impact (students and parents), so we can ensure that our deserving children are getting the best education experience possible. We also want to create a place to celebrate all of the dedicated, hard-working, amazing A+ teachers in the process. Teachers, students, and parents are creating the future of this world every day. A positive bridge of communication is a necessary step toward a brighter future, we are stronger when we work together! Listen. Learn. Grow.
